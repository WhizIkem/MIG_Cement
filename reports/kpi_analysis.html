MIG Cement KPI Analysis Report

Business Impact Summary

The KPI analysis covers 30 active sites. Using the defined operational risk indicators, 30 sites were identified as problem sites. Of these, 5 sites were classified as high-priority problem sites based on a problem score of 6 or above.

Across all sites, pour readiness failures occur on an average of 29.1% of site operating days. This indicates that planned pour requirements are not always supported by available cement inventory and deliveries. End-of-day stockouts occur on an average of 24.18% of site days, showing that several sites frequently end the day without cement buffer stock.

Overcapacity occurs on an average of 34.79% of site days, while waste-risk conditions occur on an average of 2.87% of site days. This shows that some sites carry excess cement relative to silo capacity and near-term demand.

Reactive ordering occurs on an average of 50.8% of site days, suggesting that deliveries are often triggered when opening inventory is already low. Pour disruption occurs on an average of 3.07% of site days.

Problem Site Identification

Problem sites were identified using a composite problem score. Each site received one point for each triggered KPI risk indicator: pour readiness failure, end-of-day stockout, waste risk, reactive ordering, overcapacity, low inventory, and pour disruption. Sites with a score of 1 or higher were considered to have at least one measurable operational issue. Sites with a score of 6 or higher were classified as high-priority problem sites.
